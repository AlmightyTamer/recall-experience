How it works

Three quiet acts of care.

  1. 01

    It remembers the day.

    Warm, true sentences about right now — where she is, who's coming, what's already done.

  2. 02

    It listens when she's lost.

    Clara answers in a calm voice, reading back the thread so panic never has to bloom.

  3. 03

    It stays on her device.

    Every memory lives in her hand. Private by design — dignity isn't a setting.

01

State reconstruction

A gentle anchor to right now.

Every few minutes Recall writes one warm, true sentence about the day — where she is, who's coming, what's already done. No quizzes. No alarms. Just a hand on the shoulder.

02

Clara, the voice companion

Someone to ask, any hour.

"What did I do today?" Clara listens, remembers, and answers in a calm familiar voice — reading back the day so the panic of a blank moment never has to bloom.

03

Vision-verified medication

The right pill, confirmed by sight.

Point the camera. Recall sees the tablet, checks it against the schedule, and quietly confirms — so a missed or doubled dose stops being a daily fear.

04

ACSE score & Comfort Mode

It notices the hard moments.

A quiet stability score reads the signs of a spiral. When things slip, Comfort Mode takes over — grounding words, a breath to follow, the day retold — until the ground feels solid again.

05

Apple Health vitals

Caregivers see what matters.

Heart rate, blood pressure, walking pace — pulled straight from Apple Health to the people who watch over her, so love can act on more than a phone call.

06

All on the device

Her life stays hers.

Every memory, every moment lives on the phone in her hand — not on a server somewhere. Private by design, because dignity isn't a setting.
